⚡ The Elements of Existence

The four Igbo market days embody the four classical elements of existence — Eke (Fire), Orie (Water), Afọ (Earth), and Nkwọ (Air). They align with the four cardinal directions and four divine forces, weaving a complete cosmological framework. The number four symbolizes completeness in Igbo cosmology, mirroring the universe's own quadripartite nature. This sacred rhythm has governed Igbo trade, ritual, and community life for millennia.

About the Aro (Year)

The Igbo year (Aro) consists of 13 months (Ọnwa) of 28 days each, plus one sacred intercalary day — Ubọchị Ọmụmụ. Each month contains 7 weeks (Izu) of 4 days each, cycling through EKE, ORIE, AFỌ, and NKWỌ. The Igbo Era adds 5,000 years to the Gregorian year.

📜 Cultural Note

Festival dates in the Igbo calendar are tied to specific Igbo months and market days, not fixed Gregorian dates. Actual celebrations may vary by community and region. These traditions have been practiced for thousands of years across Igboland.
